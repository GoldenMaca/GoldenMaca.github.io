Texas Sheet Cake

What does this mean to my family?

This is my families go to recipe when we have the chance. We used to make this when I was quite young. As I got older I enjoyed it more and more as my taste buds developed and still love it to this day. I would reccomend this meal especially since it makes a large portion(enough for several people) and is easy and not too time consuming.

    Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup shortening
  • 1/4 cup cocoa
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla

    Baking Instructions

  1. In saucepan, combine water, butter, shortening, and cocoa; cook and stir until boiling occurs.
  2. In a bowl mix together flour, sugar, baking soda, cinnamon and salt.
  3. Add hot cocoa mixture to flour mixture and mix until smooth.
  4. Add buttermilk, eggs and vanilla and beat well.
  5. Pour into greased 151/2 x 101/2 x 1 inch pan.
  6. Bake at 375 degrees for 20 minutes.

    Frosting

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 6 tbsp buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup cocoa
  • 1 lb powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1 tsp vanilla

    Frosting Instructions

  1. In saucepan, combine butter, buttermilk and chocolate.
  2. Cook on medium heat and stir until mixture boils.
  3. Remove from heat.
  4. Gradually blend in powdered sugar and vanilla.
  5. Beat until smooth and immediately pour frosting over warm cake.
  6. Wait for cake and frosting to cool.

Texas sheet cake is best reccomended with a side of ice cream or milk
